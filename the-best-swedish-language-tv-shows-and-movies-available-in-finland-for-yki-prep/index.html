Preparing for the YKI Swedish exam requires consistent exposure to the language, especially in real-world contexts. One of the most enjoyable ways to improve your listening skills is by watching Swedish-language TV shows and movies. Luckily, Finland offers access to a wide variety of Swedish content through both national and streaming platforms. In this article, we’ve curated a list of the best Swedish-language TV shows and movies that are available in Finland to help you hone your listening skills while also staying entertained.

1. "Solsidan" (Sunny Side)

Genre: Comedy Available on: Yle Areena, SVT Play "Solsidan" is one of the most popular Swedish comedies, offering a hilarious yet relatable look at the everyday lives of upper-middle-class families. The dialogue is fast-paced but uses common conversational Swedish, making it perfect for language learners. The humor often revolves around social situations and family dynamics, which exposes you to practical vocabulary and common phrases used in daily life. Why it’s great for YKI prep:
  • The conversational style of the show is excellent for improving your understanding of everyday spoken Swedish.
  • The use of humor makes it easier to engage with the language, allowing you to follow the dialogue even if you miss a few words.

2. "Bron" (The Bridge)

Genre: Crime/Thriller Available on: Yle Areena, Netflix Finland This Nordic noir crime series is a collaboration between Sweden and Denmark, with dialogue in both Swedish and Danish. The Swedish portions are key for learners because they present formal and clear speech, especially in investigative contexts. The show’s plot revolves around detectives from Sweden and Denmark solving cross-border crimes, giving learners exposure to both everyday and professional vocabulary. Why it’s great for YKI prep:
  • The formal and professional language used in investigations is beneficial for improving your understanding of official Swedish.
  • The show’s gripping plot keeps you motivated to listen carefully to every detail.

3. "Skärgårdsdoktorn" (The Archipelago Doctor)

Genre: Drama Available on: Yle Areena, SVT Play This beloved Swedish drama series set in the Stockholm archipelago follows a doctor and his family as they adjust to rural life. The show features clear and relatively slow dialogue, making it an excellent choice for beginners or intermediate learners. It also introduces viewers to vocabulary related to healthcare, family life, and Swedish culture in rural settings. Why it’s great for YKI prep:
  • The slower pace of conversation makes it easier to follow along and pick up new vocabulary.
  • It exposes you to Swedish spoken in both formal (medical) and informal (family) settings.

4. "En Man Som Heter Ove" (A Man Called Ove)

Genre: Comedy/Drama Available on: Yle Areena, various streaming platforms Based on Fredrik Backman’s bestselling novel, "En Man Som Heter Ove" tells the heartwarming and humorous story of a grumpy old man who unexpectedly befriends his neighbors. The film features natural, everyday Swedish, which is excellent for language learners aiming to improve their listening skills in social contexts. The dialogue is accessible to most learners, with a mix of conversational and slightly more formal language. Why it’s great for YKI prep:
  • Natural, everyday Swedish makes it easier to apply new vocabulary to real-life situations.
  • The emotional, engaging storyline keeps you invested in following the dialogue closely.

5. "Vår Tid Är Nu" (The Restaurant)

Genre: Historical Drama Available on: Yle Areena, SVT Play "Vår Tid Är Nu" is a historical drama set in post-World War II Sweden. The show follows the lives of a Swedish family running a restaurant, offering a blend of personal and professional conversations. The show uses a more formal, traditional form of Swedish in certain contexts, especially in the workplace, while also mixing in everyday colloquial speech. This gives you a broad range of language exposure, from polite formalities to casual dialogue. Why it’s great for YKI prep:
  • You get exposure to both formal and informal language, which is helpful for both the written and oral sections of the YKI exam.
  • The historical setting offers cultural context that can enhance your overall understanding of Swedish society.

6. "Babel"

Genre: Cultural Talk Show Available on: Yle Areena, SVT Play "Babel" is a Swedish talk show focused on literature, featuring interviews with authors, discussions about books, and deep dives into literary trends. For advanced learners, "Babel" provides valuable exposure to intellectual discussions and vocabulary related to literature, culture, and the arts. The show is a great way to challenge your comprehension skills while also learning more about Swedish literary culture. Why it’s great for YKI prep:
  • Provides advanced vocabulary related to literature and culture, which can help with reading comprehension.
  • Engages you with deeper, more complex discussions that improve high-level listening skills.

7. "Wallander"

Genre: Crime/Drama Available on: Yle Areena, Netflix Finland "Wallander" is another Nordic noir series, based on Henning Mankell’s famous crime novels. The series follows Swedish detective Kurt Wallander as he solves crimes in southern Sweden. The language in "Wallander" is clear and often more formal, especially during police investigations. For YKI learners, the show’s slower-paced conversations and professional tone are ideal for practicing listening skills in more structured contexts. Why it’s great for YKI prep:
  • The clear dialogue and professional language are ideal for learners aiming to understand more formal aspects of Swedish.
  • The crime-solving aspect keeps you engaged, making language learning more enjoyable.

8. "Svenska Nyheter" (Swedish News)

Genre: Satirical News Available on: SVT Play "Svenska Nyheter" is a satirical news show that presents current events with humor. While it might seem challenging at first, the show is an excellent tool for advanced learners who want to improve their comprehension of both spoken Swedish and cultural references. The fast pace of the show and its focus on current events will help learners get used to rapid conversations and real-world language use. Why it’s great for YKI prep:
  • It exposes you to current events, which can be relevant to topics in the YKI exam.
  • The humor helps you stay engaged and improves your ability to follow fast-paced conversations.

Tips for Using Swedish TV and Movies to Prepare for the YKI Exam

Watching Swedish TV shows and movies isn’t just about entertainment—it’s also a valuable learning tool. Here are some tips to maximize your learning:
  • Use Subtitles (At First): Start with Swedish subtitles if you’re still building your comprehension. As your skills improve, challenge yourself by removing subtitles altogether.
  • Pause and Repeat: If you miss a sentence or don’t understand something, pause and replay the scene. This helps reinforce your understanding of tricky phrases or accents.
  • Take Notes: Jot down any new words or phrases you hear while watching. Later, review your notes and try to use these words in conversation or writing.
  • Immerse Yourself Regularly: Make watching Swedish content part of your daily routine. The more exposure you have, the better your comprehension will become.
These Swedish-language TV shows and movies provide an excellent way to practice your listening skills in an engaging and entertaining way. By regularly exposing yourself to authentic spoken Swedish, you'll gradually improve your ability to understand different accents, vocabulary, and speech patterns, all of which are crucial for success in the YKI exam. So grab some popcorn, turn on one of these shows, and enjoy learning Swedish while preparing for your exam!
